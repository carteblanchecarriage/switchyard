Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common mechanical keyboard questions

The Basics

What makes a mechanical keyboard different from a regular keyboard? +

Mechanical keyboards use individual mechanical switches under each key instead of rubber dome membranes found in regular keyboards. This provides tactile feedback, longer lifespan (50-100 million keystrokes vs 5-10 million), and the ability to repair or customize individual keys.

Why are mechanical keyboards so expensive? +

The cost comes from individual mechanical switches, higher quality materials, programmability, and enthusiast demand. Entry-level mechanical keyboards start around $60-80, while premium custom builds can exceed $500.

Are mechanical keyboards worth it? +

If you type or game for hours daily, yes. The tactile feedback reduces typing mistakes, the durability means replacement every 10+ years instead of 2-3, and repairability saves money long-term.

Buying Guide

How much should I spend on my first mechanical keyboard? +

$80-150 is the sweet spot for first-time buyers. This gets you a quality board with hotswap sockets, good switches, and a decent case. Below $60 you sacrifice quality; above $150 you are paying for enthusiast features you may not need yet.

What size keyboard should I get? +

Full-size (100%) includes numpad. TKL (80%) removes it but keeps function row. 75% compacts further. 65% and 60% remove function row with layers. Most people do fine with TKL or 75% for general use.

Can I test switches before buying? +

Yes. Buy a switch tester (sampler kit with 4-12 different switches) for $10-20. Many online retailers sell them. This is the best way to know which feel you prefer before committing to a full keyboard.

Maintenance

How do I clean my mechanical keyboard? +

Unplug, remove keycaps, use compressed air, brush out debris, wipe plate with damp cloth. For deep clean: soak keycaps in warm soapy water, dry completely before reassembly. Clean every 3-6 months with regular use.

Can I replace individual keys or switches? +

Keycaps: Yes, if they use standard Cherry MX stems. Switches: Only if your keyboard has "hotswap" sockets. Otherwise switches are soldered and require desoldering to replace.

Technical Questions

How loud are mechanical keyboards? +

It depends on the switch. Linear switches like Cherry MX Red are quiet (40-50dB). Tactile switches like Brown are moderate (45-55dB). Clicky switches like Blue are loud (55-65dB). Silent switches with dampening exist for open offices.

What is key rollover and N-key rollover? +

Key rollover is how many keys can be pressed simultaneously. 6KRO handles 6 keys plus modifiers. N-key rollover (NKRO) handles any number. Essential for gaming; 6KRO is fine for typing.

Do I need software to customize my keyboard? +

Depends on the keyboard. Many work plug-and-play. RGB lighting and macros usually require manufacturer software. Some enthusiast boards use open-source QMK/VIA firmware for deep customization without proprietary software.
