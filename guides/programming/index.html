Buying Guide: What to Look For

Layout: 75% vs TKL vs 60%

75%: The modern standard. Fits nearly everything, keeps mouse close. Best for most programmers.

TKL: Traditional programmer favorite. Dedicated arrows and function row. If you use F-keys constantly, get this.

60%: Minimalist. Arrows on a layer. Great for Vim users, takes adjustment for others.

Switches: The Feel That Matters

For programming: Tactile switches give you feedback without the noise of clickies. Gateron Browns (budget), Holy Pandas (premium), or Boba U4Ts (modern favorite) are safe bets.

Switch weight: 55-67g is the sweet spot. Too light causes typos; too heavy causes fatigue.

Connectivity: Wired vs Wireless

Wired: No latency, no charging. Keep a wired board at your desk.

Wireless 2.4GHz: Low latency, dongle required. Good for desktop setups.

Bluetooth: Convenience, switches between devices. Slight latency noticeable for some.
