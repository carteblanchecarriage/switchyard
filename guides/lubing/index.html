Choosing the Right Lube

üõπ Krytox 205g0
Best for: Linear switches
The gold standard. Thick, buttery consistency. Apply sparingly to linear switch stems and housings. Don't use on tactiles‚Äîit'll kill the bump.
üëÜ Tribosys 3203/3204
Best for: Tactile switches
Thinner than 205g0. Preserves tactile bump while reducing scratch. 3203 is thinner (more tactile), 3204 is thicker (smoother).
üåÄ Krytox GPL 105
Best for: Springs
Thin oil for bag-lubing springs. Eliminates spring ping entirely. Can also use for switch springs if you prefer oil over grease.
Dielectric Grease
Best for: Stabilizers
Cheap and effective for stab wires. Apply to the wire ends where they clip into housings. Don't use on switch internals.
‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: Never use WD-40, cooking oil, or Vaseline. These will destroy your switches over time, attracting dust and becoming gummy.

The Process: Step by Step

1
Remove Switches from Keyboard

If your board is hot-swap: use a switch puller to remove all switches. If soldered: you'll need a desoldering pump or station. Hot-swap is much easier for beginners.

Place switches in a container‚Äîdon't lose springs or stems!

2
Open the Switches

Place the switch in the opener (or use a flathead screwdriver carefully). Push down on the four clips to release the top housing. Be gentle‚Äîbreaking clips means the switch won't close properly.

Tip: Open 5-10 switches at a time. If you open all 90 at once, springs will roll away and you'll have a bad time.
3
Lube the Springs

Method A (Bag Lubing): Drop all springs in a bag, add 3-4 drops of GPL 105, shake vigorously for 30 seconds. Fast and effective.

Method B (Brush): Use a small brush to coat each spring lightly. Takes longer but uses less lube.

Set springs aside on paper towel to drain excess.

4
Lube the Stems

Apply a very thin layer of lube to:

  • The rails (the sliding surfaces on the sides)
  • The stem legs (bottom part that hits the leaf)
  • The stem pole (center column that hits bottom)
‚ö†Ô∏è For Tactile Switches: Don't lube the legs! That's where the bump happens. Lube the rails and pole only, or you'll lose the tactile feel.
5
Lube the Bottom Housing

Apply lube to:

  • The rails where the stem slides
  • The center post (where stem pole hits)
  • The leaf contacts (lightly!)

Less is more here. Excess lube will make switches feel mushy.

6
Reassemble and Test

Place the stem in the housing, set the spring on top, align the top housing clips, and press down until it clicks. Test each switch on a spare PCB or with a multimeter.

Time Saver: Lube 10 switches, install 10, test 10. Don't lube all 90 before testing‚Äîyou might hate the feel and have to clean them all.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Too much lube: The #1 mistake. Switches feel mushy and may not register. Wipe excess with a cotton swab.
  • Lubing tactile legs: Kills the bump. Remember: linear = everything, tactile = avoid legs.
  • Skipping spring lube: Spring ping ruins an otherwise great job. Don't skip it.
  • Rushing: This takes time. Budget 3-4 hours and take breaks.
  • Wrong lube: 205g0 on tactiles = mush. Tribosys on linears = not smooth enough.
