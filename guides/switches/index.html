Mechanical Switch Buying Guide 2025: Find Your Perfect Switch

The switches under your keycaps define everything‚Äîhow your keyboard feels, sounds, and responds. Here's how to choose.

Every mechanical key press registers through a switch‚Äîthe mechanism beneath each keycap that determines:

  • How much force you need to press (actuation force)
  • How far you press before it registers (actuation point)
  • Whether you feel feedback (tactility)
  • How loud it is (noise level)
  • How smooth the press feels (linearity)

There are three main switch types, and within each, dozens of variations. This guide will help you understand what matters and recommend specific switches for your needs.

Understanding Force: The Numbers That Matter

Switch force is measured in grams (g) of force required to actuate (register a keystroke) and bottom out (press all the way down). Here's what the numbers mean in practice:

  • 40-45g (Light): Cherry MX Red, Speed Silver. Easy to press, good for rapid gaming or gentle typists.
  • 50-55g (Medium): Gateron Yellow, Cherry MX Brown. The sweet spot for most people‚Äîlight enough for long sessions, resistant enough to prevent accidental presses.
  • 60-70g (Heavy): Holy Pandas, Cherry MX Clear. Satisfying thock, intentional typing. Some fatigue over long sessions.
  • 80g+ (Very Heavy): NovelKeys Creams, Cherry MX Greens. Specialist switches. Good for heavy-handed typers.

Pro tip: If you bottom out hard when typing (hit the bottom of the keystroke), consider heavier springs or adding rubber o-rings to reduce impact.

The Switch Tester Solution

The honest truth: reading about switches only gets you so far. Feel is subjective. A $25 switch tester with 12 switches tells you more than any review.

Before committing to a full set of switches:

  1. Buy a KBDfans switch tester ($25-40)
  2. Select switches from each category (linear, tactile, clicky)
  3. Type on them for a full day at your desk
  4. Consider keycap impact‚ÄîPBT vs ABS changes sound
  5. Remember: lubed vs stock is a different experience
