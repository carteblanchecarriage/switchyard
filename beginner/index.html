So... What's the Deal With Mechanical Keyboards?

No jargon. No gatekeeping. Just answers.

The Short Version

Mechanical keyboards have individual switches under each key instead of a rubber sheet. You can feel exactly when you press the key, they last forever (literally decades), and you can customize everything.

Think of it like the difference between a cheap disposable pen and a nice fountain pen. Both write. One is just... way more satisfying.

The thing under each key that decides how it feels. Different switches = different feels. Some are smooth like butter. Some have a little "bump" you feel. Some click like a typewriter.

Easy

üî§ Keycaps

The plastic things you actually touch. Change them and your keyboard looks completely different. PBT plastic (textured, durable) is better than ABS (smooth, gets shiny).

Price: $30-150 for a full set

Medium

Case

The box everything goes in. Plastic cases feel hollow and light. Metal (aluminum) cases feel premium and make a deeper "thock" sound.

Price: Usually part of a kit ($100-400)

Easy

üìè Size / Layout

How many keys and where they are.

  • Full-size: Has number pad. Big.
  • TKL (80%): No number pad. Most popular.
  • 75%: Compact but keeps everything. Good balance.
  • 60%: Tiny. Arrow keys are on a layer.

Beginner rec: TKL or 75%. You'll get used to it fast.

Advanced

üß† PCB + Plate

The brain and skeleton. You buy these as a "kit." Look for "hot-swap" (switches pop in/out, no tools needed). Avoid "soldered" for your first board.

Price: $100-250 for hot-swap kit
